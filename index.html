What I love About Software

There’s just something about being able to create something from nothing. That feeling of “just one more thing…” that gets you excited to meet up with your computer again. Or that feeling when the best way to describe your implementation of a feature is simply “Clean”…


It’s interesting how much creativity is involved in something so logical and rule based. What is best about those two sides of software is how they come together to allow you to create something that improves people’s lives. I love when I can accomplish that.

Some Things I’ve Learned over the Years

I used to let my desire to implement features perfectly the first time slow my progress when writing code. I take pride in the quality of my work and I have a strong desire to continually learn. Why wasn’t my approach working well? I realized that I would continuously be planning and researching what the most optimal languages/frameworks, data structures, and design patterns etc were. I felt like I couldn’t move on to the next step or feature until I was sure that I had discovered the best way to architect the solution.


Then I decided to do things differently.


It’s okay to iterate! I started to journey forward with an initial implementation and would then iterate upon it.


I found that the investment of time and thought that occurred when I moved forward with an implementation triggered something in my brain; I was able to think about future architecture problems earlier on and was better able to anticipate and design for new needs because I was in a better position to “see” these concerns.


Best of all, I was able to get to a high quality solution much faster than when I was nit-picking every step. Definitely worth the change.
